How do linear approximations work?
Approximations: Shrinking the Distance

Let us try to generalize this process of producing approximations. If we want to produce an approximation of degree 1 for \( e^x \) based on the point \(x=0\), we first claim this:

  • Degree 1 Approximation

  • \( e^x \approx f(x) = c_0 + c_1 x \)

  • Degree 1 Criteria

  • \( f(0) = e^0 \)
  • \( f'(0) = \left. \frac{d}{dx} e^x \right|_{x=0} \)

Applying this here, we compute step by step:

\[ f(0) = e^0 \Rightarrow c_0 = 1 \] \[ f'(x) = c_1 \] \[ \frac{d}{dx} e^x = e^x \] \[ f'(0) = \left. \frac{d}{dx} e^x \right|_{x=0} = e^0 = 1 \Rightarrow c_1 = 1 \] \[ f(x) = 1+x \]
